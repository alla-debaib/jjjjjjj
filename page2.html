Benefits of Regular Exercise

It is widely known that regular exercise is good for health. To emphasize its importance, here are some reasons why regular physical activity can improve your health and maintain your fitness.

Reduced Health Risks

Numerous studies have shown that regular exercise can help reduce the risk of various health issues, such as diabetes, stroke, heart disease, certain cancers, depression, and obesity.

Improved Mental Health

Exercise can also help improve mental health by reducing symptoms of depression and anxiety. Regular physical activity stimulates the brain, enhancing the ability to perform tasks requiring attention, organization, and planning.

Physiological Benefits

Researchers are discovering the mechanisms by which regular exercise affects cells and molecules in the body. Studies suggest that the benefits of exercise result from small improvements in multiple physiological aspects, rather than from a significant impact on a few biological processes.

Recommended Exercise Duration

Experts recommend at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week.

Accessible to Everyone

You don't have to be a triathlon athlete to reap the benefits of physical activity. Just 20 years ago, experts in preventive medicine emphasized the importance of being active. Today, they also stress the importance of regular and moderate exercise sessions.

For example, one of our team members, Joann Mason, has contributed to studies demonstrating comparable health benefits between intense and non-intense exercise in large-scale studies. These studies, conducted from 1976 to 1909 on 123,000 female volunteers, are among the most extensive studies on women's health. By analyzing their data and other research, we can better understand the positive impact of regular exercise on overall health.
